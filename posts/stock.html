Stock development


  • ↑ Number of dwellings and population growth: new homes built and population change year on year. See the approach to estimate the historical data here.
  • Explore on this map the number of inhabitants per dwelling and municipality.
  • ↑ Housing surplus, or Housing deficit: difference between housing growth and population growth. There are two visualizations depending on the population growth:
    • By potential couples: Houses built ÷ [Population gain or loss ÷ 2].
    • By average household size: Houses built ÷ [Population gain or loss ÷ Household size].
  • ↑ Distribution of new houses built per type: Single-Family or Condominiums.
  • ↑ Construction period: number of today existing dwellings per period of construction.
  • The green bar is a final estimate based on the current pace of construction. If the pace remains as in 2021 (95,000 per year), the number of houses built between 2021 and 2040 will be ≈1,800,000.
  • ↑ Cement consumption, Spain. Consumption and real price correlate positively.
    • Left axis (line): real price in euros per square meter.
    • Right axis (bars): cement consumption per year, in metric tons.
